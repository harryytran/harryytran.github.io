Mexico, officially known as the United Mexican States, is a country located in the southern part of North America. Here are some key points about Mexico: Population and Urbanization As of 2024, Mexico's population is estimated to be around 132,529,000, making it the 10th most populous country in the world. It has the largest population of Spanish speakers globally. Nearly four-fifths of Mexicans live in urban areas, with Mexico City being one of the most populous cities and metropolitan areas in the world 23

Geography and Borders

Mexico covers an area of 1,972,550 kmÂ² (761,610 sq mi), making it the world's 13th largest country by area. It shares borders with the United States to the north, Guatemala and Belize to the southeast, and has maritime borders with the Pacific Ocean to the west, the Gulf of Mexico to the east, and the Caribbean Sea to the southeast23.

Mexico is a federal republic with a two-house legislature consisting of the Senate (128 members) and the Chamber of Deputies (500 members). The country is divided into 31 states and Mexico City, which serves as the capital and has a special political status. Mexico City is in the process of gaining greater autonomy and is transitioning to become a federal entity with its own constitution and legislature34.

Economy Mexico has a significant economy, ranking 15th in nominal GDP and 11th by purchasing power parity. The economy is driven by industry (33%), services (63%), and a smaller agricultural sector (4%). Key industries include automotive, oil, electronics, financial services, and tourism. Despite economic growth, Mexico faces challenges such as social inequality, poverty, and systemic corruption23.
